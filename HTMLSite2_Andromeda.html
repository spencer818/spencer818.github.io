Details

The Andromeda Galaxy goes by many names. It is also designated as M31, NGC 224, UGC 454, PGC 2557, 2C 56, CGCG 535-17, MCG +07-02-016, IRAS 00400+4059, 2MASX J00424433+4116074, GC 116, h 50, Bode 3, Flamsteed 58, Hevelius 32, Ha 3.3, and IRC +40013. But Andromeda is the most common and least technical name.









The Andromeda Galaxy is, by defenition and by name, a massive cluster of stars, planets, gasses, etc. It is believed that, at the center, is a large, dense star cluster orbiting a supermassive black hole.






Andromeda has an estimated mass of around 1.5e+12 M☉ (1.5 trillion solar masses), with an estimated 1 trillion stars, and is roughly 220,000 lightyears across. It has an apparent magnitude of 3.4, making the brighter central regions barely visible with the naked eye. The below image is a representation of what Andromeda would look like with the naked eye if it was much brighter. It would be more 6 times the size of a full moon.


Location



Andromeda is our closest galactic neighbor. Even so, it is still 2.54 million lightyears (a smooth 28.5 trillion year's drive) from Earth.


Andromeda can be found in our night sky in the Andromeda Constellation, just off of the arm towards the constellation Cassiopeia, at the coordinates (RA: 0h 42m 44s, Dec: +41° 16m 9s).
